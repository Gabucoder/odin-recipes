jollof_rice

Jollof rice is a flavorful and vibrant West African dish made with long-grain rice cooked in a rich, spicy tomato-based sauce. It combines tomatoes, onions, peppers, and a mix of aromatic spices to create its signature taste and color. The dish is known for its smoky, slightly sweet, and savory flavors, often enhanced with chicken or vegetable broth. Jollof rice is typically served as a main dish, accompanied by sides like fried plantains, grilled chicken, fish, or a fresh salad. Its bold taste and versatility make it a celebrated meal across many African countries.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 (14.5 ounce) cans stewed tomatoes
  • ½ (6 ounce) can tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon chopped fresh rosemary
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 (3 pound) whole chicken, cut into 8 pieces
  • 1 cup uncooked white rice
  • 1 cup diced carrots
  • ½ pound fresh green beans, trimmed and snapped into 1 to 2 inch pieces
  • ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg

Steps

  1. Pour oil into large saucepan. Cook onion in oil over medium-low heat until translucent.
  2. Stir in stewed tomatoes and tomato paste, and season with salt, black pepper, cayenne pepper, red pepper flakes, Worcestershire sauce and rosemary. Cover, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat, stir in water, and add chicken pieces. Simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Stir in rice, carrots, and green beans, and season with nutmeg. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover, and simmer until the chicken is fork-tender and the rice is cooked, 25 to 30 minutes.
